    <div class="card">
      <h3>ERGMs and network inference</h3>
      <p>
        I study statistical inference for exponential-family random graph models, with emphasis on scalable
        likelihood approximation, Bayesian priors, MCMC-based computation, and variational estimation.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="card">
      <h3>Bayesian latent variable models</h3>
      <p>
        I work on hierarchical and longitudinal psychometric models using efficient Bayesian estimation,
        including data augmentation and auxiliary-variable methods.
      </p>
    </div>
    <div class="card">
      <h3>Simulation benchmarking</h3>
      <p>
        A recurring theme in my research is benchmarking estimators under controlled simulation settings to
        understand tradeoffs among accuracy, runtime, and scalability.
      </p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="section-panel">
    <h2>Research perspective</h2>
    <p>
      Much of my work asks the same broader question: how can we make sophisticated statistical methods more
      computationally practical without sacrificing inferential quality? That question has led me to problems in
      Bayesian computation, network modeling, psychometrics, and large-scale simulation design.
    </p>
