I am a PhD candidate in Statistics at the University of California, Los Angeles. My work spans data science, machine learning, Bayesian modeling, experimentation, and statistical computing.

My recent experience includes developing gradient boosting models at Farmers Insurance, building large-scale research pipelines at UCLA, and conducting methodological research in network modeling and Bayesian inference.

## Focus

- Machine learning and statistical modeling
- Bayesian modeling, variational inference, and MCMC
- Experimentation, simulations, and causal analysis
- Large-scale data workflows in Python, R, SQL, and AWS

## Current roles

### Data Science Intern, Farmers Insurance
- Developed scalable gradient boosting models using XGBoost and LightGBM to predict auto accident risk.
- Engineered features from 80+ million row Snowflake SQL pipelines and trained models in AWS SageMaker.
- Tuned hyperparameters with Optuna cross-validation and used SHAP for interpretability.

### Doctoral Researcher, UCLA Statistics & Data Science
- Developed advanced statistical models for social and economic network data using exponential-family random graph models.
- Derived a Bayesian reference prior for ERGMs and implemented Metropolis-Hastings MCMC and variational Bayes approaches.
- Built a simulation benchmarking framework for evaluating likelihood approximation procedures.

### Research Data Scientist, UCLA School of Education & Information Studies
- Led work on a U.S. Department of Education research project on hierarchical latent-space models for psychometric applications.
- Developed Bayesian auxiliary sampling methods using Pólya-Gamma data augmentation.
- Reduced runtime through model redesign and Bayesian sampler optimization.
