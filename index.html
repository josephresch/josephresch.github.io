About

I'm a statistician with a Ph.D. from UCLA. My dissertation developed Bayesian methods for social and economic networks, the kind of models that describe how friendships and trade relationships form. As a research data scientist in psychometrics, I built hierarchical Bayesian models that track students' latent skills over time, and released the method as an R package.

At Farmers Insurance I trained gradient boosted risk models that now run in production. I'm at my best where all of this comes together: experimentation and causal inference to learn what works, Bayesian modeling to stay honest about uncertainty, and machine learning to act on it.
