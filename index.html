About

I am a statistician with a Ph.D. from UCLA, where my dissertation developed Bayesian inference methods for exponential-family random graph models. My research centered on the degeneracy problem in network models, and I care most about turning statistical methodology into tools that people can actually use.

I build production machine learning systems on large-scale data. Most recently I developed risk models on more than 80 million rows at Farmers Insurance, using gradient boosting, Snowflake pipelines, and AWS SageMaker. I work primarily in Python and R.
