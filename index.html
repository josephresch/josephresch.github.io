I am a Statistics PhD candidate at UCLA with experience across machine learning, Bayesian modeling, computational statistics, and large-scale applied data science. My work sits at the intersection of rigorous statistical methodology and real-world decision-making, spanning academic research, psychometrics, and industry applications in insurance risk modeling.

My recent work includes developing scalable gradient boosting workflows at Farmers Insurance, building Bayesian latent variable models for educational measurement at UCLA, and advancing computational methods for exponential-family random graph models (ERGMs), including variational inference and MCMC-based approaches.
